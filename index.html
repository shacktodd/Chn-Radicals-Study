Don't just recognize it — recall it.

Recognizing a character is easy; producing it from memory is the exam. This companion is built around retrieval practice and spaced repetition — the two most proven ways to make vocabulary stick — and it builds week over week.

Core method · Active recall + spacing

The Recall Trainer.

You're shown the meaning; you produce the character from memory, then check yourself and rate how it went. Words you miss come back fast; words you know get spaced out over days. This is the engine that turns recognition into recall.

Build up · Cumulative sentence practice

From words to sentences.

Retention sticks best in context. Recall the missing word in a real sentence, or rebuild a sentence from scrambled tiles — both pull from everything you've studied up to this week, so earlier vocabulary keeps working for you.
